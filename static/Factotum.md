# Factotum : Project Management for Polymaths
Traditional project management tools are not for soloists with diverse capabilities: they often optimize for team collaborations. You have a time budget, a supervisor you answer to (or many who answer to you!), discussion sections, etc. These functionnalities are simply redundant for a soloist.

What's more, as I have tried many different solutions, I realized that project management for soloist is a management of stamina than a management of time. It should optimize to prevent burning you out than telling you that there's a deadline coming and whips at your back. This stamina management philosophy becomes the backbone of Factotum. 

To be more specific, Factotum believes that each soloist's abilities are each a battery pack. Pouring too much attention on tasks that require using a same ability will drain the ability's battery. And the battery is drained, it causes burn out. Thus, Factotum will try to ask what you feel about your abilities before proposing you a task. If you feel like your inkpot is dried, or that you have worked too many tasks recently that drains your inkpot, Factotum will politely ask you whether you want something different. This way the journey is more likely to be exciting. Whereas in traditional project management, you are always assumed to be capable of doing a very specific thing (and that may be why you feel burnt out sometimes during your day jobs!).
